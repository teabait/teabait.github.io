Tea Ho is a writer

and queer Viet-American refugee whose notable accomplishments include surviving life in a frat house for over two years, getting inducted into a now-defunct secret society, and receiving a phone call from her uncle — after he died.

Tea is currently a fellow in Lena Waithe’s inaugural Hillman Grad Mentorship Lab as well as a mentee in Giant Leap's Development Accelerator Program. She is the writers' assistant for The Sympathizer on HBO.

In her past lives, Tea worked as a web developer, designer, and wedding photographer. She writes and performs at the Magnet Theater in NYC. Last year, Tea was a finalist in the HBO Women in Comedy Festival’s pitch competition, a semi-finalist for WeScreenplay’s Diverse voices, and a semi-finalist for Ben Cory Jones’ (Insecure, Boomerang) TV writing program. Tea's writing balances dark truths with levity to tell socially-conscious stories. Her goal is to make people laugh enough to let their guard down and hear stories from people they might otherwise tune out.
