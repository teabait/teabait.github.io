Tea Ho is a writer

and queer Viet-American refugee whose notable accomplishments include surviving life in a frat house for over two years, getting inducted into a now-defunct secret society, and receiving a phone call from her uncle — after he died.

In her past lives, Tea worked as a web developer, designer, and wedding photographer. Now she writes for CNT Productions, a female and non-binary led comedy collective with a social justice tilt. She has written and performed in sketch and improv shows at Magnet Theater and the People’s Improv Theater in NYC. Her work often features reasonable people trying their best to survive outlandish circumstances. Tea's goal is to make people laugh enough to let their guard down and hear stories from people they might otherwise tune out.

Free Ideas
